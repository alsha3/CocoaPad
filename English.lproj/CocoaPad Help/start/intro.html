Introduction

Why another word processor? Most, like Microsoft Word, are big, honking, feature-ridden programs that have a lot of unnecessary bloat.

If you need a fairly lean word processor that has a bit more than TextEdit can give you, then use CocoaPad—the free and simple alternative.

Keyboard shortcuts and the Command key

Whenever we say "Command-S", for example, we mean hold the Command key (the key next to the spacebar with a cloverleaf and an Apple logo on it), and press the S key. Then let go of both keys. "Command-click" means hold Command, click the mouse, then let go of Command. The same thing applies to all of the "modifier" keys surrounding the spacebar. For example, an often-used command is "Control-click", which means, you guessed it, hold Control, click the mouse, then let go of Control.

What about TextEdit?

You probably have a perfectly self-respectable text editor called TextEdit sitting in your Applications folder. What's wrong with that?

Well, TextEdit may have its own wrap-to-page editing mode, but other than that, CocoaPad betters TextEdit in its cool little "utilities" that do even cooler things. Plus, there are little things here and there that make CocoaPad great, like how CocoaPad applies your preferences when you set them. And does TextEdit have an automatic backup feature?

Of course, CocoaPad is nowhere from perfect. More features will be added with future releases. Perhaps we'll get wrap-to-page sometime soon…
