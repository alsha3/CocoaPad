Changing your document's background color

Does that dull, white-washed canvas that you print your words on start to seem a little, well, not fun? Wouldn't it be cool to make your document look like the Milky Way for a change. You can!

To change the background color:

  1. Pull down the Format menu, and select Change Background Color.
  2. The color picker will appear. Choose the color you want your text to be.
  3. Click the close button to close the color picker.

Warning: This is only temporary. The background color is not saved, so once you close the document, the background color will not reappear when you open it again.
If you change the background color, your text color also changes. This is a bug with Apple's color picker, and it is something out of our control. You'll simply have to set the text color again after setting the background color. Sorry!
You can set a default background color by making a trip to CocoaPad Preferences and clicking on the "Default document background color" color well. There, you can change the background color of all CocoaPad documents (so your documents can always look like the milky way!).
