The toolbar

CocoaPad has a cool little toolbar on top of each document's window. It's kind of like a shortcut for doing the things you would do most often, like Bold, Save, Print, etc.

You can even customize what's on the toolbar, and how it's arranged.

To customize the toolbar:

  1. Select Customize Toolbar… from the Window menu, or press and hold Control, click on a blank spot on the toolbar, and select Customize Toolbar…. If you have the Customize toolbar item on the toolbar, you can also click that.
  2. Drag items around to rearrange them. To get rid of an item, simply drag it off the toolbar. To add an item, drag the item from the customization palette to the toolbar.
  3. Click Done.

Of course, you don't have to like the toolbar. If you want it to go away, click the "pill" button on the document window's title bar (which looks like this: ), or pull down the Window menu and select Hide Toolbar. To show the toolbar again, simply repeat the process (notice that the menu item now says Show Toolbar).
You can rotate through different ways of how the toolbar should look by Command-clicking the "pill" button on the document window's title bar.
